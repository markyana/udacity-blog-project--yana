3 ways to take a great photo

by

Yana Markova

Self-Employed Web Designer
Forever a student. Educated in Entrepreneurship and practicing Wed Design. Currently studying Web Development.

April 30th, 2021

Everyone takes photos now, but how do you know you’re taking good ones? Here are some simple rules you can follow to make your photos stand out in the crowd.

1. Are you the only one?

When taking a photograph, look around, are there other people taking photographs of the same thing? If yes, then you’re not unique and neither is your photograph. This is a crowd-pleaser, and won’t be anything special.

When taking photographs, try to follow the rule of finding something that no-one else is seeing. Use your personal and unique vision to form a scene. Be different and be yourself, and that will make your photographs stand out.

2. Follow the rule of thirds

This is very important. Make sure you’re framing all your photographs properly. There are many great videos online that explain how to use the rule of thirds. Take a look and stick to the rules, the difference will be amazing.

3. Look at what you’re taking a picture of

Make sure you know what you’re doing. Look first, see the scene, then snap. And when you snap, position yourself so that you’re capturing exactly what you saw - keep the camera straight, don’t cut off body parts or corners, keep the lines balanced.

These are my top three ways of getting into a focused mindset. I hope they help you as well!
